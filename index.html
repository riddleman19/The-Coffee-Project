What's special about hand-roasted, sensory-evaluated coffee? ‚ñº

Most commercial coffee is roasted by machine‚Äîtemperature sensors and pre-set profiles, no human judgment. Our SCA-certified roaster evaluates every roast by eye (color development), smell (aroma progression), sound (first crack timing), taste (cupping protocol), and heat management. What is the "development time" between first and second crack? ‚ñº

After the first crack (at ~196¬∞C), the beans are technically "drinkable" but still developing flavor. Development time (6‚Äì8 minutes for specialty coffees) is when Maillard reactions continue, caramelization deepens, and the roaster can fine-tune sweetness vs. acidity balance. We stop our roasts in this window to preserve the bright, complex volcanic character while building caramel sweetness. This is where the magic happens.

What is a Q-Grader and why does it matter? ‚ñº

A Q-Grader is the international gold standard for coffee sensory evaluation, certified by the SCA (Specialty Coffee Association). It requires 6 days of intensive training in cupping protocols, flavor descriptors, defect detection, and green bean agronomy. Our roaster is Q-Grader certified, meaning they've proven expertise in objectively evaluating coffee quality and can identify exactly what terroir and roast contribute to the cup. It's a credential most coffee drinkers will never encounter‚Äîbut they'll taste the difference.

How does volcanic soil affect the flavor? ‚ñº

Volcanic soil is rich in minerals (potassium, phosphorus, magnesium) that enhance plant health and bean density. Higher altitude (2,000m+ in volcanic regions) slows cherry ripening, developing more sugars and complex acids. The result: volcanic coffees are known for bright acidity, stone fruit/floral notes, and exceptional clarity. Our roaster's agronomy training means they understand these terroir characteristics and roast to highlight them‚Äînot mask them.

How do you ensure consistency batch-to-batch? ‚ñº

Small-batch roasting (50kg at a time) allows our expert roaster to adjust in real time rather than follow a rigid machine-driven profile. Every batch is logged (time, temperature, aroma cues, color changes), cupped (tasted) during cooling, and scored against SCA standards. While no two roasts are identical‚Äîeach bean batch has its own character‚Äîthis method ensures consistent quality, clarity, and expression of terroir across all orders.
